Vertical Farms Now Supply 8 Percent of Singapore's Leafy Greens

Singapore's investment in vertical farming technology is paying off as indoor farms now account for roughly 8 percent of the city-state's leafy green vegetable production. The government's 30-by-30 initiative, which aims to produce 30 percent of nutritional needs locally by 2030, has attracted over $200 million in private investment since 2019.

The largest facility, operated by VertiVegies, occupies a converted industrial warehouse and produces 2 tonnes of lettuce, spinach, and kale daily using stacked hydroponic systems under LED lighting. Energy costs remain the primary challenge, though recent partnerships with solar providers have reduced operating expenses by 25 percent.

Nutritional studies comparing vertical farm produce with conventionally grown equivalents found comparable vitamin and mineral content, with significantly lower pesticide residues. The controlled environment also eliminates seasonal variation, providing consistent year-round supply to local supermarkets and restaurants.
